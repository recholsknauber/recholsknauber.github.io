I am going to seminary because I love it. It isn't better than what I'm doing now, or lesser than what I'll do after. I have my own understanding to challenge and broaden, along with a cohort to commune with and love. Jesus Christ still has a mountain of teaching that I've only begun to climb, and I'm excited to offer the devotion and practice I've gained from Zen.

After seminary my goal is to give everything I've gained. I don't know yet what shape this will take–ordination or otherwise, but I'm confident in these first steps. SFTS appeared in my life at the perfect time and I'm prepared to go all in with my professors and fellow students. Christianity has so many beautiful offerings to the world, and I'm fascinated to see how it continues to blossom over the next decades. I have no plans to change it; only to live it by embodying the Trinity to the best of my ability.

My weakness will most likely come as a lack of vision; I've historically not been a person with a clear path. My strengths cultivated around this are faith and bravery, being able to maintain my diligence despite the unknown. I love work, and will plan on leaving a lasting mark on SFTS in the shape of my heart.
